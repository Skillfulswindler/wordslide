Privacy Policy

Last updated: 12 July 2026

The short version. Wordslide has no accounts and no sign-in, and we never ask for your name, email or address. Your game progress stays on your device. Three third-party services do receive limited technical data so that ads, purchases and basic play statistics can work at all: Google AdMob (ads), RevenueCat and the app stores (purchases), and Google Firebase Analytics (anonymous play statistics). We do not sell your data. Everything below is the detail.

1. Who we are

Wordslide (“the game”, “we”) is an independent mobile game published by Nick Potter. If you have any question about this policy or about your data, email nick.potter.one@gmail.com and we will respond within 30 days.

2. What stays on your device and is never sent anywhere

Your scores, best word, level and world progress, XP, coins, daily streak, achievements and settings are stored locally on your device only (in the app’s own local storage). We cannot see them, and they are not backed up to us. Deleting the app deletes them permanently — there is no cloud save, so there is also nothing for us to hand over or lose.

3. What is collected, by whom, and why

We do not operate our own servers or database. Data is collected by these providers:

ServiceWhat it receivesWhy
Google AdMob
(ads)		 Advertising identifier (IDFA on iOS / Advertising ID on Android), IP address, coarse location derived from that IP, device model and OS version, and your interactions with ads (shown, tapped, rewarded video completed). To show interstitial and rewarded ads and to detect click fraud. If you consent, ads may be personalised. If you decline, you still see ads, but non-personalised ones.
Google Firebase Analytics
(play statistics)		 A random app-instance identifier (not tied to your identity), device model, OS version, country derived from IP, and gameplay events such as level started, level completed, run score, power used, ad shown and purchase completed. To understand which levels are too hard, where players stop playing, and whether the game is working. You can turn this off — see section 6.
RevenueCat and
Apple / Google
(purchases)		 A random anonymous purchase identifier, your purchase receipt, and which items you bought. Your payment card and billing address go to Apple or Google only — they are never sent to us and we never see them. To validate that a purchase is genuine, to remove ads for people who paid for that, and to restore your purchases if you reinstall the game.

4. What we never collect

  • Your name, email address, phone number or postal address.
  • Your contacts, photos, camera, microphone, or files.
  • Precise GPS location. (Coarse country/region is inferred from IP by the providers above.)
  • Payment card details.

We do not sell personal data, and we do not share it with data brokers.

5. Consent, and your choices in the EEA, UK and California

If you are in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the game shows you a consent form (Google’s User Messaging Platform) the first time it runs, before any ad is requested. You choose there whether ads may be personalised. You can change that choice at any time from Settings inside the game.

On iOS you may also see Apple’s “Allow tracking?” prompt. If you decline it — or if you decline personalised ads on the consent form — the advertising identifier is not used to track you across apps.

If you are a California resident, you have the right to know what is collected, to request deletion, and to opt out of “sharing” for cross-context behavioural advertising. Declining personalised ads on the consent form, and turning off analytics in Settings, exercises that opt-out. You may also email us to make a request.

6. How to turn things off

  • Analytics: Settings → turn off “Share anonymous play data”. This stops collection; it does not merely hide it.
  • Personalised ads: Settings → Privacy options (the consent form), or your device settings (iOS: Settings → Privacy → Tracking; Android: Settings → Google → Ads).
  • All ads: the one-off “Remove ads” purchase removes them entirely.
  • Everything: deleting the app removes all local data.

7. Children

Wordslide is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect personal information from them. The game is not configured for child-directed treatment with our ad provider. If you believe a child has provided personal information to us, contact us and we will delete it.

8. Data retention and security

We hold no personal data ourselves, so we have nothing to retain. Our providers retain data under their own policies and periods. All data leaving the app is sent over encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS).

  • How Google uses data from apps that use its services: policies.google.com/technologies/partner-sites
  • RevenueCat: revenuecat.com/privacy

10. Changes

If we change what is collected, we will update this page and change the date at the top. Material changes will also be noted in the app’s update notes. This policy reflects what the app actually does today — if you find a discrepancy, that is a bug and we want to hear about it.
